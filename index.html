What's Chizzle?

Creating bevelled text in Blender is unexpectedly a big hassle. Default bevel options are too limited, produce bad topology, and for some reason, almost always return non-manifold geometry with artifacts on most fonts.

If you have ever searched for "how to make Chiseled text" in Blender, you will find answers asking you to either model the geometry manually in Edit Mode, or do it in Adobe Illustrator and export it back to Blender. Both of these solutions are destructive, not scalable, and take tons of time.

Chizzle makes this process insanely simple. It harnesses the power of Geometry Nodes to give you a customizable, procedural, and non-destructive solution that eliminates the hassle entirely.

Endless Customization

Because Chizzle is powered by Geometry Nodes, you get full control directly inside the Modifiers tab. Every setting is neatly organized into categories, keeping your interface clean and your workflow stress-free.

Chizzle gives you control over just about everything in your model. You can tweak the simple stuff, like tightening up letter spacing or pumping up the extrusion depth, or go deep on those procedural noise settings if that's your thing. So if you're after a razor-sharp modern style or a crusty, ancient stone feel, the interface is so straightforward you'll have your perfect look locked down in seconds.

Why Chizzle?

Chizzle is designed to feel like a native Blender feature. It integrates seamlessly into the Add menu and the modifier stack. Whether you are doing motion graphics, architectural visualization, or stylized art, Chizzle handles the typography so you can focus on the composition.
